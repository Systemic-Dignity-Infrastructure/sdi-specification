# Risk Register

This register consolidates the structural risks facing the Systemic Dignity Infrastructure into a standard engineering risk table. Each risk is assessed for likelihood, impact, and mitigation strategy.

---

| ID | Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation | Validation | Owner |
|:---|:-----|:-----------|:-------|:-----------|:-----------|:------|
| RK-01 | Federal Medicaid waiver (§1115) not renewed | Medium | Critical | Develop contingency fiscal model; diversify revenue streams (philanthropic, state general fund) | Monitor CMS renewal timeline; engage waiver advocacy coalition | Executive Operations |
| RK-02 | Incumbent service providers block deployment via procurement gatekeeping | High | High | Route funding through private capital and managed care billing rather than county procurement contracts | Track procurement interference; document Olson-predicted opposition patterns | Executive Operations |
| RK-03 | NIMBY opposition delays or blocks site acquisition | High | Medium | Rely on AB 2011 by-right authorization to eliminate discretionary review; ground floor commons design to convert facility into neighborhood asset | Monitor community opposition; track AB 2011 legal challenges | Executive Operations |
| RK-04 | Civil liberties legal challenge to compelled care pathway | Medium | High | Four-condition conjunctive threshold; LPS Conservatorship (WIC §5350) pathway with existing constitutional standing; Anti-Detention Covenant | Track ACLU/Disability Rights California litigation; maintain independent neuropsychiatric evaluation protocol | Principal Systems Architect |
| RK-05 | Pod Steward recruitment failure (insufficient qualified candidates) | Medium | Critical | Competitive compensation; partnership with community college and social work programs; internal training pipeline | Recruitment pipeline metrics; retention rate tracking | Executive Operations |
| RK-06 | Cooperative micro-economy fails to bridge Return Deficit | Medium | High | Conservative wage ratio targets; phased market exposure; Tenancy Bridge Guarantee provides buffer | Quarterly cooperative payroll vs. market wage analysis | Principal Systems Architect |
| RK-07 | Adaptive reuse costs exceed new construction baseline | Low | High | Rigorous site feasibility assessment; standardized conversion specification; master lease negotiation | Per-unit cost tracking; abort threshold defined pre-conversion | Executive Operations |
| RK-08 | Singular Prototype fails verification thresholds | Medium | Critical | Prototype is designed to produce data regardless of outcome; negative result falsifies specific mechanisms, not entire framework | Independent third-party evaluation at 90, 180, 365 days | Principal Systems Architect |
| RK-09 | Political capture degrades framework integrity | High | Critical | Administrative Firewall (GOVERNANCE.md); split-authority architecture; automated disqualification via PILOT-SPECIFICATIONS.md | Governance log audit; decision trail review | Principal Systems Architect |
| RK-10 | Resident participation in cooperative governance insufficient | Medium | Medium | Progressive onboarding; peer mentorship; governance training integrated into RDI curriculum | Monthly participation rate tracking; threshold at 70% | Principal Systems Architect |
| RK-11 | AB 2011 / AB 2243 by-right authorization overturned | Low | Critical | Maintain compliance with all four qualifying conditions; engage legislative monitoring coalition | Track California Supreme Court rulings; maintain fallback permitting strategy | Executive Operations |
| RK-12 | Dunbar-scale cohort sizing empirically invalidated | Low | Medium | Prototype measurement at defined windows; cohort size is a tunable parameter, not a fixed constant | 90, 180, 365-day relational stability data | Principal Systems Architect |
