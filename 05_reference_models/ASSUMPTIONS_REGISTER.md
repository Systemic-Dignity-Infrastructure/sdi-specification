# Assumptions Register

This register documents every major assumption underlying the Systemic Dignity Infrastructure. Each assumption must be explicitly acknowledged, monitored, and validated during pilot implementation. If an assumption is invalidated, the dependent design component must be revised.

---

| ID | Assumption | Category | Dependent Component | Validation Method | Risk if Invalid |
|:---|:-----------|:---------|:--------------------|:------------------|:----------------|
| A-01 | Sufficient surplus commercial real estate exists in target metropolitan areas for adaptive reuse | Real Estate | MDI Tower Architecture; Surplus Capacity Activation | Commercial vacancy rate analysis; broker market survey | Deployment timeline extends beyond 180 days; alternative site acquisition required |
| A-02 | Medicaid managed care plans will recognize homelessness as a qualifying condition for Enhanced Care Management | Fiscal | CalAIM billing architecture; Efficiency Surplus Model | DHCS policy guidance review; managed care plan contract verification | Revenue model collapses; alternative funding pathway required |
| A-03 | Federal Medicaid waiver (Section 1115) will be renewed beyond December 2026 | Fiscal | CalAIM billing; long-term fiscal sustainability | Federal waiver renewal tracking | Entire CalAIM revenue stream eliminated; fiscal model requires restructuring |
| A-04 | Qualified Pod Steward candidates exist in sufficient numbers at proposed compensation levels | Staffing | RDI Production Conditions; 1:7 ratio | Recruitment pipeline analysis; labor market benchmarking | Pod Steward ratio cannot be maintained; RDI layer fails |
| A-05 | Residents will voluntarily participate in cooperative governance at sufficient rates | Behavioral | Cooperative Sovereignty; Ostrom's Principles | Pilot observation; participation rate tracking | Cooperative governance fails; external management required |
| A-06 | AB 2011 / AB 2243 by-right authorization will survive legal challenge | Legal | Surplus Capacity Activation; deployment timeline | Legislative tracking; court ruling monitoring | Discretionary review reinstated; 18-36 month permitting delays |
| A-07 | Adaptive reuse construction costs remain below new construction baseline | Fiscal | Efficiency Surplus; deployment cost model | Per-unit cost tracking during pilot conversion | Cost advantage eliminated; fiscal model requires revision |
| A-08 | Average occupancy rate in converted towers will sustain ≥ 85% | Operational | Efficiency Surplus; revenue model | Monthly occupancy audit | Revenue shortfall; operating deficit |
| A-09 | Dunbar-scale cohort sizing (groups of ~7) produces measurable relational stability | Behavioral | RDI Architecture; Pod design | Prototype measurement at 90, 180, 365 days | Cohort sizing requires empirical recalibration |
| A-10 | LPS Conservatorship pathway (WIC §5350) provides sufficient legal authority for the compelled care threshold | Legal | Phase Zero compelled pathway | Legal review; court petition outcomes | Compelled pathway unavailable; voluntary-only model required |
| A-11 | External labor market Return Deficit is bridgeable via cooperative wage rates | Economic | EDI Architecture; Tenancy Bridge Guarantee | Cooperative payroll vs. market wage tracking | EDI layer fails; alternative reintegration pathway required |
